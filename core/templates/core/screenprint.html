What is this project about?

Our New Media and Tech for Social Change is a technology skill building class learning this like how to photoshop, code, editing and etc. The goal for this project was to design a custom frame of your photoshopped work that started from a photo and was screen printed onto fabric, paper and a card board box.

My Design Process

When this project in New Media and Tech for Social Change, my first idea was to have a girl sitting in a chair inside a hand heart in another universe saying girls world. It later got changed from me realizing the amount of screens i needed and how the colors worked for this project. creating our idea we used photoshop inserting the image we took of a body part and making something that revolves around it. In adobe photoshop we used different layers and brushes to separate our designs for how many frames you planned on using.

Photo creation

A teammate of mines took a couple photos of my desire which was my hand in a heart. I chose my picture to have a straight shot angle, narrow depth field with the aperture setting being f4 and my shutter being 1/1000. Once I figured out which one I liked most I later inserted it in adobe photoshop I cut out the background and inserted a girl sitting down inside of the heart. Short after that I had to change my colors to black and pink because I wanted to use two frames. When screen printing there are only 6 colors yellow,black,white,red,magenta and cyan.

Summary of Building

After figuring out my final design and using graphic organizers we used an app called adobe illustrator to separate and measure the size of our frames. Once that was done and all of the dimensions were made we got our wood and started measuring for the frames then later started cutting using a saw machine. Once all 8 of my pieces of wood were cut since I was making two frames I started gluing the wood and stapling them together. During this stage you will need a partner to staple the wood while you holding the glue and two wood pieces together. After the glue was dry I cut the screen 1.5 inches bigger then my frame on each side. Stretching and stapling the screen over the wood making sure it was tight, this is also a two person job one has to stretch the screen and the other has to staple. Next we inserted a final image onto a app connected to the printer and it cut it out on a special paper. After the printer was done cutting we use tools to peel out the lined pieces that the paint need to go through. If you had two screens you needed to be extra careful when peeling so you donâ€™t peel out a piece from the other screen and mess it up. Once done we put our design on the screen white side up, put heat protection paper on top and used baby iorns and melted on screen down into the paper. We later peeled the clear paper off and it was time to start screen printing. We used clamps to hold our screen down to the table and taped a piece of paper under our frame. The first frame I got black paint and put above my design, placed the screen down, got the scraper and started pulling the paint down across the screen once or twice. I reptead this step a couple times on paper until I liked it, then later did it on a piece of fabric and cardboard. I repeated those steps with my other color but mixed white and magenta to get the pink I wanted. After that I got my draft from my black screen lined it up with my pink frame as close as possible and put my pink on top of the black doing that with each paper, cardboard and fabric.
